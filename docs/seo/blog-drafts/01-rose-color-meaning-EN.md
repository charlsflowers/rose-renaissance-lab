## Body (markdown)

Roses say what words sometimes can't — but only if you pick the right color. Before you order, here's what each rose color means, so the bouquet matches the message.

## Red roses: love and passion
Red is the classic. A bouquet of **red roses** is the universal sign of romantic love, which is why it's still the most-requested anniversary and Valentine's gift. If your message is "I love you," red is the safe, timeless choice.

## White roses: purity, respect and new beginnings
**White roses** carry purity, reverence and fresh starts. That's why they fit weddings and christenings, but also sympathy and remembrance — a quiet, elegant way to say "I'm with you."

## Pink roses: gratitude, admiration and sweetness
**Pink roses** sit between friendship and romance. Lighter pinks read as grace and gentleness; brighter "hot pink" reads as gratitude and joy. A great pick for a thank-you, a first date, or a "thinking of you."

## Yellow roses: friendship and joy
**Yellow roses** mean friendship, warmth and good cheer — no romantic pressure attached. They're the right call for a friend, a graduation, or simply to brighten someone's day.

## Black roses: farewell, mystery and bold statements
True black roses are painted, and they carry drama: endings, mystery, or a striking, modern look. They work for a dramatic gift, a themed event, or a goodbye.

## Blue, orange, purple and green roses
- **Blue roses** suggest the rare and the impossible — a one-of-a-kind person.
- **Orange roses** mean enthusiasm and energy.
- **Purple roses** signal enchantment and love at first sight.
- **Green roses** stand for renewal and good fortune.

### Which color should you send?
- Romance → red
- Wedding / sympathy → white
- Thank-you / friendship → pink or yellow
- Bold statement → black
